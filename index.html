Engineering Portfolio

Hello, I'm Ellen! I am a sophomore at Stanford University, majoring in Mechanical Engineering. I am passionate about creative problem-solving and pushing the boundaries of innovation.


At Stanford, I'm pursuing my interests in several projects along with my classes. On the Student Space Initiative (SSI), I am the systems lead for the IREC competition rocketry project, New Collins. I also work on my own R&D Projects, which currently include a minimum Diameter rocket and a spaceshot attempt. Aside from rockets, I'm also interested in robotics. In the CHARM Lab, I'm currently working on electronic design and integration for an underwater robotics project. Preprofessionally, I'm the Academic Development Chair for the Society of Undergraduate Mechanical Engineers (SUME), Stanford's branch of ASME, where I plan and execute academic and research related events for MechE undergrads.


When I'm not tinkering away on my variety of projects, I enjoy a variety of hobbies including playing card games, swimming, bird-watching, hiking, and painting.
