Fountain Hopper Ignitor: Detailed Overview

Context

Fountain Hopper is the Stanford Student Space Initiative (SSI) Rocket Team's rocket for the Collegiate Propulsive Lander Challenge, a new rocketry competition that aims to take new innovations in industry's self-landing rockets to the collegiate level. We aim to complete a series of milestones that will result in a self-landing rocket. We are using a hybrid propellant system to accomplish this. This is SSI's first time attempting hybrid rocketry, and weâ€™ve encountered many learning opportunities thus far.

Content

One of the parts of the Fountain Hopper enginer I am in charge of is devloping the ignition method. I researched different ignitors, including attending a lecture about the different styles during the 2025 Liquid Propulsion Symposium. I settled on using a small COTS motor to light and melt the solid paraffin wax part of our hybrid engine. The small COTS motor is set off using an e-match. I am optimizing for a burn time of around 10 seconds, along with a flame that spreads in a way that allows it to reach and ignite the paraffin grain. I am 3D printing the casings + nozzles using PLA, with the goal that it may melt away as the motor ignites. Currently, I am experimenting with different nozzle types to find the nozzle that allows for the best spread and burn time.
