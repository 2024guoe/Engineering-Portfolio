Level 2 High-Powered Rocket: Detailed Overview

Context

The L2 Certification is a National Association of Rocketry (NAR) certification that allows certificate holders to launch rockets with I, J, and K class motors with insurance. To attain this certification, I will pass a test to demonstrate my knowledge of the laws and regulations pertaining to high power rocketry as well as technical rocket knowledge and then successfully launch and recover my L2 rocket.

Content

I am designing my L2 rocket with the goal to learn as much as possible within the world of amateur rocketry. I will be designing and coding an avionics (AV) component that allows me to GPS track my rocket’s flight and have dual-deploy parachutes. Additionally, I will 3D print an AV bay. As I also want to learn how to work with composites, I’ll be adding carbon fiber lay-ups on my fins. My phenolic body tube will be 66mm in diameter and have a 54mm motor mount, and I will be creating my own fiberglass nose cone.
