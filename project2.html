Fountain Hopper Injector: Detailed Overview

Context

Fountain Hopper is the Stanford Student Space Initiative (SSI) Rocket Team's rocket for the Collegiate Propulsive Lander Challenge, a new rocketry competition that aims to take new innovations in industry's self-landing rockets to the collegiate level. We aim to complete a series of milestones that will result in a self-landing rocket. We are using a hybrid propellant system to accomplish this. This is SSI's first time attempting hybrid rocketry, and we’ve encountered many learning opportunities thus far.

Content

On Fountain Hopper, I helped design and test the injector. I researched different showerhead-style injectors previously used in liquid and hybrid rockets to determine possible orifice arrangements on the injector plate and best orifice diameters. My team is using the Homogeneous Equilibrium Model to model two phase flow through our propulsion system, and I use those equations to program optimal injector arrangements based on different initial assumptions. Using OnShape, I CADed potential designs and ran careful analyses using various softwares. My current injector is a four ring, 42-orifice brass plate arranged in a scattered fashion. As my team begins to transition to the testing phase, I'm also researching different failure modes and cold flow testing set ups.

Conclusion

As the team conducts cold flow testing, hydrostatic testing, static fires, and hot fires in the coming weeks, I will be improving the design of the injector as needed.
